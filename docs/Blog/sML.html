Introduction to Scientific Machine Learning

In the physical sciences Machine Learning (ML) has been widely adopted to analyze data and develop powerful prediction machines and classifiers. Especially deep neural networks (DNNs) are being used increasingly as it seems to be a promising universal approximator. Yet, although their mechanics, their rules are clearly defined, their dynamics, especially during training, are poorly understood. Now researcher outside the the ML community are starting to find interest in learning approaches. Notably physicists are in desperate need of new techniques to model n-body problems e.g. in astrodynamics or quantum mechanics, as well as modelling biophysical systems.

"While many attribute the success of deep learning to its black-box nature, the key advances in deep learning applications have come by developing new architectures which directly model the structures that are attempting to be learned." - Universal Differential Equations for Scientific Machine Learning
